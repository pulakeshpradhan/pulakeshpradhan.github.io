Agricultural drought monitoring using Google Earth Engine: a study of Paschim Medinipur district, West Bengal

Book: Modern Cartography Series Year: 2024 Google Earth Engine

This study analyzes agricultural drought in Paschim Medinipur, West Bengal, using Google Earth Engine with satellite and climate datasets from 2002–2022. It assesses precipitation variability, drought indices, vegetation responses, and climate trends, highlighting extreme droughts, shifting water availability, and resilience improvements through NDVI and land surface cooling.

Application of analytic hierarchy process for mapping flood vulnerability in Odisha using Google Earth Engine

Book: Google Earth Engine and Artificial Intelligence for Earth Observation Year: 2025 Google Earth Engine

The analytic hierarchy process (AHP) is a structured technique to support complex decision-making by giving weights to the parameters of a problem into a hierarchy of criteria. This study demonstrates the use of AHP in Google Earth Engine to identify vulnerable areas for flood hazards in Odisha. Key parameters selected for this study are elevation, rainfall distribution, land cover, slope, Topographic Wetness Index (TWI), Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI), Soil, and Distance from water bodies identified through an extensive literature review. Pairwise Comparisons Matrix (PCM), Priority Weights (PW), Principal Eigen Value (PEV) and Consistency Index (CI) were calculated between parameters. CI value for this study is 0.062, indicating high consistency in subjective judgments with 4.446% Consistency Ratio (CR). The weighted overlay method in Earth Engine integrated these spatial layers based on the AHP criteria weights. The resulting maps provide a robust delineation of areas highly prone to flooding. Ganjam, Puri, and Balasore districts are associated with high flood vulnerability. Whereas the Bhadrak, Jajpur, Koraput, Sundargarh, and Cuttack district are associated with moderate vulnerability and Bolangir, Kalahandi, and Dhenkanal with low vulnerability zone.
