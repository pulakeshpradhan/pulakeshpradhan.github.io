Publisher Submission Guide: Avoiding Fees

Most top-tier journals are Hybrid, meaning they offer both a Subscription (Free for authors) and an Open Access (Paid) option. To avoid paying Article Processing Charges (APCs), you must select the correct option during submission.

Tipüí° Key Terminology
  • Subscription / Traditional: The reader pays (via university library), so the author pays nothing.
  • Gold Open Access: The author pays an APC to make the article free for everyone.
  • Hybrid: A journal that offers BOTH options. You must strictly choose ‚ÄúSubscription‚Äù to publish for free.

How to Select ‚ÄúNo-Fee‚Äù by Publisher

Publisher System Name Action Required for Free Publishing
Elsevier Editorial Manager Look for the ‚ÄúPublishing Options‚Äù step after acceptance. Select ‚ÄúSubscription‚Äù (often the default is Open Access, be careful).
Springer Editorial Manager / SNAPP During submission, you will be asked about Open Access. Select ‚ÄúNo, I want to publish my article via the traditional subscription model‚Äù.
Wiley ScholarOne In the license signing phase (Author Services), choose the ‚ÄúOnlineOpen: No‚Äù or ‚ÄúStandard License‚Äù option.
Taylor & Francis ScholarOne After acceptance, select ‚ÄúStandard Article‚Äù (Green OA compliant) instead of ‚ÄúGold Open Access‚Äù.
IEEE ScholarOne IEEE often has page charges for long papers (e.g., >10 pages). Standard publication is free within page limits; avoid selecting ‚ÄúOpen Access‚Äù.

üõ°Ô∏è Submission & Safety Tips

Before submitting your manuscript, ensure you verify the journal‚Äôs credibility and understand its metrics.

üìä Understanding Metrics

Journals are ranked by Impact Factor (IF) within their subject categories (e.g., Geography, Environmental Sciences).

  • Q1 (Top 25%): Highest prestige and visibility. Often hardest to accept.
  • Q2 (Top 25-50%): Excellent quality, balances visibility with slightly higher acceptance rates.
  • Q3/Q4: Good entry points for niche topics or early-career research.

üö© Avoiding Predatory Journals

Be cautious of ‚ÄúClone Journals‚Äù that mimic the names of reputable titles.

  1. Verify Indexing: Always check the journal on the official Master Journal List (MJL).
  2. Check ISSN: Ensure the ISSN on the journal website matches the one on MJL.
  3. Red Flags: Avoid journals promising ‚ÄúReview in 3 days‚Äù or ‚ÄúGuaranteed Publication‚Äù.
